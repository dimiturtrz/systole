### Q1 · Magnet — 4/5
**Asked:** why does the superconducting magnet hold its field with no power supply, why isn't it a dangerous short, what's a quench?
**Answer:** no resistance below a certain temperature; short *is* a danger if temp rises (heat → fire); quench = fast failsafe that drains the current.
**Grade:** Right on persistent current (R=0 → no supply) and the failure mode (lose superconductivity → resistance → heat). **Missing:** *why it's safe normally* — at R=0 there's **no heat AND no driving source** (the two things that make a short dangerous are both absent; energy just sits stored in the field). Quench also = **helium boils off/vents**.

### Q2 · Protons — 2/5  ⚠ weakest
**Asked:** why hydrogen protons specifically (not electrons / other nuclei); where does M point at rest; why undetectable yet?
**Answer:** radio band is convenient; non-ionizing (electrons need higher); the work principle needs the RF to send a nudge — not enough to just lie in the magnet.
**Grade:** Those points are true but secondary. **Missed the core:** hydrogen because it's **abundant (water ≈60% of body)** and a **single *unpaired* proton**; electrons are mostly **paired → spins cancel**; nuclei with paired spins are **MRI-invisible**. Also missed: at rest **M points along B₀ (z)**, undetectable because it's **static — no rotation, no induction.**

### Q3 · Resonance & safety — 3/5
**Asked:** how does a B₁ millions of times weaker than B₀ tip M; why can't RF break bonds?
**Answer:** resonance — it targets the frequency; the energy is insufficient otherwise to break bonds.
**Grade:** Right direction, thin. Add **mechanism**: B₁ at the Larmor frequency gives **timed pushes that accumulate** (pushing a swing); it never overpowers B₀. Safety reason: **RF photon ~10⁻⁷ eV ≪ bond ~eV → non-ionizing**, only slight heating (SAR).

### Q4 · Slice selection — 3/5
**Asked:** how to excite one *oblique* plane; what sets thickness?
**Answer:** gradient dictates the direction Larmor frequency changes; RF targets that center frequency to start imaging.
**Grade:** Core correct. **Missing:** oblique slice = **combine x/y/z coils** to aim the gradient any direction; **thickness = RF bandwidth ÷ gradient strength.**

### Q5 · Signal / echo — 3.5/5
**Asked:** how do protons produce a signal; why only the tipped ones (use "induction")?
**Answer:** protons tipped off B₀ by the resonant wave oscillate more than the others, net effect induces an electrical field read by the receiver.
**Grade:** Good — tipped → precess → induce. Sharpen *why only tipped*: untipped M is **static along B₀ → no changing field → no induction**; only **transverse/spinning** magnetization induces. (Pedantic: induces a **voltage/current in the coil**, not "an electric field.")

### Q6 · Encoding — 3.5/5
**Asked:** why does frequency get an axis in one read but phase needs many repeats; how many for 256 rows?
**Answer:** need two differentiators for 2D; frequency gives a k-space row but can't disambiguate which part contributes; need 256 columns for 256 rows.
**Grade:** Good intuition (repeats needed; ~256). **Missing the reason:** **one frequency "ruler" labels one axis at a time** — two live gradients blend axes ambiguously. Precise count: **256 phase-encode reads** resolve 256 rows; frequency gives the 256 columns **within** each read.

### Q7 · k-space → image — 4/5
**Asked:** what is one k-space point; how does the grid become an image; why complex?
**Answer:** like audio — each point is a visual wave of repeating stripes (rotated/exposed/silent); borders = thin stripes (high freq), middle = big ones; complex because it's a 2D vector (direction + magnitude); we phased the protons to get the readings.
**Grade:** Strong on *what points are* + geometry (center=broad, edges=detail). **Missing:** *how the grid becomes the image* — **inverse 2D FFT = scale + shift every stripe pattern by its vector and sum them all.** Phase is essential because it **places** features (lose phase → lose structure).

### Q8 · Cardiac timing — 4/5
**Asked:** image takes ~0.6 s–minutes but the heart moves — how get a sharp movie?
**Answer:** heart motion repeats → many chances to record a given stage (assuming constant beat); if not constant, tie to ECG and match beats to phases.
**Grade:** Core correct (periodicity + ECG gating + phase matching + variable-rhythm handling). **Missing:** **segmented** acquisition (a few lines per beat, stitched over ~10–20 beats), **breath-hold**, and **cine** = repeat for ~20–30 phases → movie.
