### Q1 · 0.99 everywhere but 85% right — name it; confidence or calibration? — 5/5
**Answer:** overconfidence / miscalibration; a calibration problem — a model should know how good it is.
**Grade:** ✓ Calibration: the confidence exists, it just doesn't match accuracy.

### Q2 · Where does softmax entropy concentrate, why useful? — 4.5/5
**Answer:** at the boundary — where it's supposed to be uncertain.
**Grade:** ✓ Useful because uncertainty lands where error actually is (honest map) + it's
a sanity check (uniform/off-boundary uncertainty → something's wrong).

### Q3 · MC dropout vs ensemble — source of disagreement? — 4/5
**Answer:** ensembles obvious; dropout hides a bit of info — if that flips the answer,
no "mountain of evidence" for the voxel.
**Grade:** Dropout nailed. Cleaner contrast: **ensemble = disagreement across separately
trained models** (different parameter sets); **dropout = across sub-networks of ONE
model** (cheap approximation of the same).

### Q4 · ACDC 0.87 → Philips 0.75: broken? name it; what changed? — 3/5
**Answer:** domain drift; likely the units.
**Grade:** "Domain shift" ✓. **Not units** — MRI is unit-less. The **intensity
distribution / appearance** shifted (Philips noise/contrast/reconstruction) → **out-of-
distribution**. And: the model **isn't broken** — it just never saw Philips. Generalization
limit, not a bug.

### Q5 · E4→E5 bridge: what should a good system DO on the weird scanner? — 5/5
**Answer:** report lower confidence → flag for human; the repeated dropout checks
disagree.
**Grade:** ✓ Uncertainty spikes on OOD input → low confidence → flag, don't auto-report.
The dropout variance is the OOD detector.

### Q6 · Two things separating ACDC-0.87 from a clinical tool (not Dice) — 2.5/5
**Answer:** handling uncertainty / knowing what we don't know.
**Grade:** That's **one** thing said twice. Need a **second distinct** axis: **regulatory
validation** (CE/FDA prospective), **multi-vendor/site validation** (M&Ms), **reference-
label quality** (single expert), or audit trail/monitoring.
