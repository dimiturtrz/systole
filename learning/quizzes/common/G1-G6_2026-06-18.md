### Q1 · Why EF survives wrong-but-constant spacing, mL doesn't — 2/5
**Answer:** same model, same boundary bias.
**Grade:** That's the *model-bias* cancellation (also true) — but the question was
**spacing**. Spacing is a constant factor (dx·dy·dz) on **both** volumes; in the ratio
(EDV−ESV)/EDV a common factor **cancels** (scale-invariance). mL has no ratio → needs
real spacing.

### Q2 · Simpson vs voxel-counting vs Riemann — 5/5
**Answer:** same thing; also the same as a Riemann sum.
**Grade:** Correct.

### Q3 · Why the probability field gives a better surface — 3/5
**Answer:** a good U-Net gives e.g. 0.2 = "mostly background here" (4 classes, simplistic).
**Grade:** Right seed (soft = partial occupancy). Missed the mechanism: soft values let
marching cubes **linearly interpolate the 0.5 crossing to a sub-voxel position** →
smoother. Hard 0/1 pins the crossing at the edge midpoint.

### Q4 · ~5k verts from 12k voxels — 2.5/5
**Answer:** mesh is triangles, endpoints bounded by #voxels; we model few big shapes.
**Grade:** "Bounded by voxels" isn't the reason (you had this last quiz). Core: **only
boundary cubes generate vertices**; interior/exterior cubes make none → **verts ∝
surface area, not volume.** Vertices sit **on edges** at the boundary.

### Q6 · Median flat / max jump + DCM — 3/5
**Answer:** muscle contracts the wall but most is unaffected so thickness stays; DCM →
low compression so max would be low too.
**Grade:** Non-uniform insight right (bulk → median flat; localized → max). But the max
*did* jump (12.9→15.6); the point is **flat median is consistent with DCM's weak
contraction** (a healthy heart thickens the whole ring more).

### Q7 · What thickness catches that EF can't — 5/5
**Answer:** scars / localized problems; AHA-17 maps 17 regions to check.
**Grade:** Correct — regional/focal disease, the AHA 17-segment map.

### Q8 · Dice 1.0 / HD 208 / HD95 — 3/5
**Answer:** Hausdorff is max, Dice is overlap; HD95 = 95th-percentile error, big but not outlier.
**Grade:** Right on *what* they are; missed *why these values*: Dice=1.0 because the far
blob **doesn't change overlap** with the big heart; HD=208 = the **one farthest point**.
And **HD95 drops the top 5%** → for a single speck it reads **small**, not "big."

### Q9 · Systematic 1mm under-segmentation — which catches it — 5/5
**Answer:** none; no single number catches it.
**Grade:** Correct (Dice tolerates, HD95 small, EF cancels the ratio).

### Q10 · What rigid registration wrongly reports — 2.5/5
**Answer:** local movement.
**Grade:** Rigid *can't capture* local movement — so what does it **report**? ~zero /
uniform motion: it dumps the contraction into residual → the strain map comes out
**empty**. It erases the signal.

### Q11 · MI not SSD cross-modality — 1/5  ⚠ weakest (wrong question)
**Answer:** CT is static, a rigid transform would say the heart is just scaled, motion
not captured.
**Grade:** That's the registration-type question, not this one. Cross-modality is about
**intensity matching**: SSD assumes same-tissue→same-intensity, but cross-modality the
mapping is **arbitrary/inverted** (bone dark in MRI, bright in CT). **MI** ignores
intensities, measures **statistical dependence** ("does the MRI value predict the CT
value?"). Re-read G5.

### Q12 · SSM = mean + ? / why plausible — 4/5
**Answer:** spread — PCA of the variations across matched points, encoded as features; a
linear model aggregated over many hearts.
**Grade:** Yes — **mean + PCA modes**. Missed *why guaranteed plausible*: a fit is only
**mean + combinations of variations seen in real hearts** → it stays inside the space of
real anatomy, can't produce an impossible shape.
