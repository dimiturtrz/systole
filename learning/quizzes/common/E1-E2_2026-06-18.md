### Q1 · Dice mechanics (pred 120, truth 100, overlap 80) — 2.5/5
**Answer:** 80/140; over-segment / high recall, lower precision (false positives).
**Grade:** That's **IoU** (overlap/**union** = 80/140), not Dice. **Dice = 2·overlap/
(pred+truth) = 160/220 = 0.727.** Error-type **right**: 40 FP vs 20 FN → over-segments →
precision < recall.

### Q2 · Dice size-bias (1px error: fat disk vs thin ring) — 2/5
**Answer:** bigger disk gets lower Dice, error rate is lower (1px).
**Grade:** **Backwards** — the **thin ring** scores lower, and your own "error rate lower
for the disk" proves the disk is *higher*. 1px is a tiny fraction of a fat disk's area
but ~50% of a 2px ring's width. **High perimeter-to-area → boundary error dominates →
low Dice.** This is why thin RV < fat LV-cav before any skill gap.

### Q3 · Bad Dice but good HD95 — 2/5
**Answer:** systematic error but balanced.
**Grade:** Didn't land. **Thin/small structure shifted ~1px:** boundaries stay close
(HD95 tiny) but the shift wipes out overlap (Dice tanks) — same physics as Q2.
Clinically: **right place, wrong area** — trust location, distrust volume.

### Q4 · Bias vs precision (A: bias 0, LoA ±10; B: bias −5, LoA ±2) — 3/5
**Answer:** changes if you account for bias; otherwise A better (unbiased on average).
**Grade:** Got the key instinct (correctability matters) but picked wrong: **B is better.**
**Bias is correctable** (subtract −5 → B becomes bias 0, LoA ±2); **random spread is not**
— A's ±10 is stuck. Precision is the irreducible currency; bias is a removable offset.

### Q5 · The reference has error (inter-rater ±3%) — 4.5/5
**Answer:** a model trained on human labels can't beat the human; 3.1% is good then.
**Grade:** ✓ The **error floor**: labels certain only to ±3% → model can't meaningfully
beat that. **MAE 3.1% ≈ the inter-rater floor → as good as the labels allow**; further
"gains" may just overfit one annotator. (So sub-SOTA Dice may not be the bottleneck — the
labels are.)

### Q6 · Dice and EF decouple — 4/5
**Answer:** (a) maybe middle frames good not ED/ES; or opposite errors — ED too big, ES
too small, error grows. (b) overlaps bad but ratio preserved; EF is a simple metric.
**Grade:** Decouple insight **excellent** (ignore "middle frames" — EF uses only ED/ES):
- (a) **anti-correlated** errors (over-seg ED + under-seg ES) both inflate EF → bad EF,
  great per-frame Dice. ✓
- (b) **correlated** bias → cancels in the ratio → good EF, mediocre Dice. ✓

## Gaps to revisit (study targets)
1. **Dice ≠ IoU.** Dice = 2·overlap/(pred+truth); IoU = overlap/union. *(Q1)*
2. **Thin structures score LOWER Dice** for the same boundary error (perimeter/area). *(Q2, Q3)*
3. **Bad-Dice + good-HD95 = thin structure, small shift** (right place, wrong area). *(Q3)*
4. **Bias is correctable, spread is not** → tight-biased beats loose-unbiased. *(Q4)*

**Verdict:** systems reasoning is strong (error floor Q5, anti-correlated decoupling Q6,
correctability instinct Q4). Every miss was **mechanics** — Dice-vs-IoU, thin-vs-fat
direction, what-shifts-overlap. The rigor gap is precise computation/direction, not
understanding. Drill: "what's in the numerator vs denominator, and what's thin vs fat?"
